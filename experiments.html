Experiments

Graphics demos built in OpenGL

Perlin Noise

A series of random noise functions are added together to produce a natural looking terrain. To add to the sense of height, valleys are darkened and blurred to produce a simple ambient occlusion effect. The gif below uses a gaussian kernel to blur the terrain.

Diffusion-Limited Aggregation

An structure is formed from a cloud of randomly moving particles. When a particle touches part of the structure, it becomes part of it. Natural “coral” columns and patterns are formed with this process, since the aggregation is limited by the diffusion of the particles.

Reflection Mapping

A reflection texture is mapped to the surface based on orientation and normals.

Postprocess HDR Bloom and Reflection Mapping

Reflection mapping applied to a model instead of a surface. HDR bloom is applied by blurring the brightest parts of the rendered image.

Postprocess HDR Bloom, Reflection Mapping, and Shadow Mapping

A teapot with shadow mapping, too. Fancy!

Volumetric Rendering and Julia Set Fractal

A slice of the Julia set fractal is calculated in OpenCL, along with an occlusion map which darkens denser areas of the generated image. Generating the set without GPU acceleration would take tens of minutes - OpenCL calculates the set and occlusion data in seconds, then passes the data to OpenGL for realtime interactive rendering using a 3D texture.

Cel Shading

A simple GLSL postprocessing effect.
